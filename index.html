About

Inspired by the complexity and spontaneity of generative music-making software, I was quickly drawn into the world of computer programming.

I’ve since found so much to engage with as a programmer outside of my musical interests. Web development/design, in particular, is a skill set that allows me to continuously flex my curiosity while offering the opportunity to adapt my knowledge base to a wide variety of industries.

Projects

Sit

‘Sit’ is a simple, user-friendly meditation timer. It works as a traditional countdown timer, with the option to add brief ‘interval’ alarms/reminders at timed spaces throughout the session. These serve as reminders to focus on the object of meditation.

Learn more...

Sit

The idea for ‘Sit’ arose upon noticing an apparent lack of simple, free, mobile-friendly, web-based mindfulness meditation timing apps designed with user experience in mind. Those that do exist tended to be clunky and awash in a “new-age” aesthetic that seemed off-putting to the average user. My intention was to fill this gap with a tool that was neutral in appearance and immediately navigable to users with little, if any, tech experience.

User research

User research (email surveys to regular meditators in the Nashville community) suggested that simplicity and mobility were indeed top priorities. There was a general sense that subscription-based meditation apps had too much on offer (ie social connectivity and course offerings that ultimately distracted from the practice itself). Users indicated an interest in a structured experience, but not necessarily one governed by specific courses or guided meditations. There was also interest in a mechanism with which to track individual progress over time.
